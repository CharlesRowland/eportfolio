Black and Yellow Team - Black Box - Claim Evidence Reasoning

By Charles Rowland

The purpose of this investigation is to figure out a way that a Black Box could have input water inside of it, but a different output water as you add more. Our team has designed, annotated, and reconfigured models to build a potential model of how this black box works.

CLAIM

The mechanism inside the Black Box is a tank in which water goes in, and the tank is also connected to another tank, which water also fills, when the water gets to a certain point, a portion of the water will exit the tank.

EVIDENCE

The mystery black box, where water is the input, and a different amount is the output. What we have noticed about the data of the mystery black box, is that when you run trials with so much input water, nothing comes out of the tube, but when you fill the tank enough, a lot more will come out. If you perform more trials after this, water will come out but only 60-65% of the water that went in might come out. To figure out how this box works, we ran multiple tests and observed water output and concluded that it had to be a tank that fills and something to trigger the water to exit the tank or it gets to a limit for water to exit the tank. We created a model involving 2 tanks, one filled with water, and once it gets to a limit it brings water into the 2nd tank, and when that gets to a limit, water will exit the tank. Our first trial of the model we had 250 ml input with a 0 ml output, the second trial we had 500 ml input with 250 ml output in 21.75 s. Our 3rd trial we had 250 ml output in 17 s.

REASONING I

We used a physical model to demonstrate how we thought the black box could operate. Models can represent how you want a real design to work, except in a simpler conception, and can be either bigger or smaller than the true thing. There are many variants of models such as digital, 3d, and drawing on paper. Our model helped us get the message of our design and gave us a fast building time.

REASONING II

Our evidence has shown that our model was able to accurately depict what could be inside the Mystery Black Box on a smaller scale. We ran multiple tests that are similar to the differences to the Black Box Tests, which could lead us to conclude that our design fits with the boundaries of the design.

REASONING III

If an alternate claim, such as a different mechanism for the box, were to be correct, then it could differ from the initial data, the water input and output would be much different and the different operations of the mechanism could affect the time by a bit as well. This means my other claim would be flawed and inaccurate according to this data.
